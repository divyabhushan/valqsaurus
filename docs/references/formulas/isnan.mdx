id: isnan
title: ISNAN
---
The `ISNAN` function checks for error and returns a default value if a value in the node is an error.

## Syntax
```javascript
ISNAN (value1,value2)
```

## Arguments
`value1` – The node that is to be checked for error values.

`value2` – The value which is to replace the error values.

## Return value
value2 in the place of Error.

## Example

Consider the nodes Total Cost and No. of units to have the periods values as shown below

| Period        | Jan | Feb | Mar | Apr | May | Jun | Jul | Aug | Sep | Oct | Nov | Dec |
|---------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Total Cost    | 100 | 150 | 200 | 100 | 300 | 250 | 300 | 0   | 225 | 300 | 0   | 300 |
| No. of units  | 10  | 15  | 10  | 10  | 15  | 25  | 15  | 0   | 15  | 15  | 0   | 15  |

In the below example, Cost per unit is calculated by dividing Total Cost and No. of units. 

Before applying `ISNAN`:

| Period        | Jan | Feb | Mar | Apr | May | Jun | Jul | Aug | Sep | Oct | Nov | Dec |
|---------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Cost per unit | 10  | 10  | 20  | 10  | 20  | 10  | 20  | #   | 15  | 20  | #   | 20  |

Cost per unit returns Error(#) for Aug and Nov, the error is to be replaced with 0.

To achieve this, the formula should be 
```javascript
ISNAN(Total Cost/No. of units,0)
```

Applying applying `ISNAN`:

| Period        | Jan | Feb | Mar | Apr | May | Jun | Jul | Aug | Sep | Oct | Nov | Dec |
|---------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Cost per unit | 10  | 10  | 20  | 10  | 20  | 10  | 20  | 0   | 15  | 20  | 0   | 20  |
